Jesus College May Ball is currently looking for motivated and enthusiastic workers to help make this year’s Ball a huge success.

There are a variety of roles available, including Food and Drink workers, Security Stewards, and Entertainment Runners, as well as the opportunity to take on a leadership position as a Team Leader or Court Supervisor.

We do not operate a 'half-on, half-off' employment policy like many May Balls, and workers are offered a competitive rate which starts at £70 for the night and increases for certain roles.

Applicants must be willing to work as part of a team and should demonstrate charisma and enthusiasm in order to make the 19th of June a spectacular night. Previous experience in hospitality or May Balls, whilst beneficial, is not essential.

This is a great opportunity to be a part of one of the most exciting events of the year whilst earning some money.

Please apply before the 12th February for the first consideration. Interviews will be held on the 18th and 19th of February, as well as the evening of 20th February.

To apply, please complete our online application form below. Please feel free to email us for more information at staffing@jesusmayball.com.

Team Leader - £90

Team Leaders will oversee all the workers for one section of the Ball, (e.g. the food or on-call team). They are responsible for ensuring that everybody working on their team is confident in what they are doing and all work is completed to a satisfactory standard. In addition, Team Leaders are responsible for signing their team in and out at the start and end of the Ball.

We are looking for people who:

  • Have a high attention to detail.
  • Can work well under pressure.
  • Can use their initiative.
  • Have excellent communication skills.
  • Can motivate and manage a team.

Previous leadership experience is essential for this role.

Court Supervisor - £85

Court Supervisors are responsible for ensuring everything operates smoothly in their designated court or area. This involves communicating with Team Leaders and relevant members of the committee when any issues arise or extra staffing is required. Court Supervisors are also responsible for signing in and out workers at the beginning and end of their breaks.

We are looking for people who:

  • Can work well under pressure.
  • Can use their initiative.
  • Have excellent communication skills.
  • Can motivate and manage a team.

Previous leadership experience is essential for this role.

Food Drinks Worker - £70

Food and drink workers will be rotated through a variety of positions throughout the night. The positions mainly involve preparing and serving food and drink, as well as ensuring food and drink stalls are well stocked.

We are looking for people who:

  • Are friendly.
  • Can be professional when interacting with guests.
  • Can adapt quickly to new tasks.

Previous experience in hospitality or catering is certainly helpful.

Security Steward - £70

Security stewards are responsible for reporting anyone attempting to enter the Ball without a valid ticket; however, at no point will they be expected to directly confront or remove any unauthorised persons themselves. Security stewards are also responsible for the health and safety of guests, which involves fire stewarding and reporting any guests that behave in an inappropriate or dangerous manner.

We are looking for people who:

  • Are attentive and hard-working.
  • Can be professional when interacting with guests.

Ents Runner - £70

The role of the ents runners is to escort acts to the correct stage at the correct time, and to help ensure that the entertainment schedule for the night runs smoothly.

We are looking for people who:

  • Show good timekeeping and organisational skills.
  • Can stay calm under pressure.
  • Are friendly and personable.

On-Call Team - £70

Workers on the on-call team will be allocated different responsibilities throughout the night, depending on where staffing is needed. This may involve responding to emergencies that arise. The role may also involve stepping in where certain teams or areas are understaffed. This is perhaps one of the most interesting roles due to its varied nature, but also requires a cool head and an ability to adapt.

We are looking for people who:

  • Can work well under pressure.
  • Can use their initiative.
  • Can adapt quickly to new tasks.
  • Can respond quickly to instructions.

Glass Collection Worker - £70

The glass collection team are responsible for collecting dirty glasses, washing them using the college dishwashers and returning them to the bars and drink stalls. Workers will be rotated to different areas throughout the night in order to ensure they can experience as much of the ball as possible. This role is essential for the running of the bars and the appearance of the Ball.

We are looking for people who:

  • Can be professional when working around guests.
  • Are attentive and hard-working.

Litter Collection Worker - £70

The litter collection team collect rubbish throughout the night and ensure it is disposed of appropriately. Workers will be rotated to different areas throughout the night in order to ensure they can experience as much of the ball as possible. This role is essential to ensure the Ball is kept clean for guests and to minimise clear-up required after the Ball has finished.

We are looking for people who:

  • Can be professional when working around guests.
  • Are attentive and hard-working.

Sound Monitor (2 positions available) - £100

The sound monitors are responsible for monitoring and recording the sound levels around the Ball throughout the night. They will liaise with the Technical Logistics May Ball Committee member, performing acts, the production company and Cambridgeshire Council, and must ensure acts do not exceed the allowed sound levels.

We are looking for people who:

  • Are assertive.
  • Can be organised and efficient when recording measurements.

Please be aware that Jesus College May Ball does not operate a half-on half-off system. You may be invited for an interview and we will contact you if your application is successful, providing further information about your role. The standard shift time for workers is from 19:00 - 05:00 with two unpaid 30 minute breaks, although these might be altered depending on your role. Please note that all workers will be expected at Jesus College by 16:00 on the evening of the ball (Monday 19th June 2017) to allow time for sign-in, a walk-through of the grounds, briefing, training, and to help in the final set-up for the ball. All workers will be expected to sign in again at 05:00 before leaving the ball. Workers must also attend one short training session at Jesus College, provisionally scheduled for Sunday 11th June (1pm-3pm), Monday 12th June (10am-12am) and Tuesday 13th June (6pm-8pm). Attendance at one of these sessions is compulsory. All employees at Jesus College May Ball will be required to read a contract (which stipulates the terms and conditions of employment), sign it and return it to us. We will also require a passport photo of each worker and two £75 cheques made payable to "Jesus College May Ball". These are to cover any minor or major breaches of contract but will otherwise be destroyed after the ball on fulfilment of the employment.
